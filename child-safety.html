Child Safety Policy

Feetzer is committed to protecting children from sexual abuse and exploitation. This page describes our standards, practices, and how to report concerns.

Our Commitment

Feetzer strictly prohibits any content, behaviour, or interaction that constitutes child sexual abuse or exploitation (CSAE). This includes but is not limited to child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, solicitation, or any other form of exploitation or abuse of minors.

We have zero tolerance for violations of this policy. Any account found to be involved in such activity will be immediately and permanently suspended, and all relevant content will be removed without warning.

Prevention Measures

  • All users must agree to our Terms of Service, which explicitly prohibit harmful or illegal content involving minors
  • Users can report any profile, message, or content directly within the app at any time
  • Users can block other users instantly from any interaction screen
  • All user reports are reviewed promptly by our moderation team
  • Accounts involved in CSAE-related violations are permanently banned
  • All illegal content is removed immediately upon identification
  • We cooperate fully with law enforcement and relevant national and regional authorities

In-App Reporting

Every user of Feetzer has access to in-app tools to report child safety concerns:

  • Report a user profile using the three-dot menu on any profile screen
  • Report a specific message or content from within any chat screen
  • Block a user immediately from any interaction screen

All reports are taken seriously. Reports related to child safety are treated as highest priority and escalated immediately. We do not tolerate CSAM or CSAE under any circumstances.

Reporting to Authorities

Feetzer complies with all relevant child safety laws. Where required by law, we report confirmed cases of child sexual abuse material (CSAM) to the relevant national and regional authorities, including:

  • The National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline where applicable
  • Local law enforcement agencies in the relevant jurisdiction
  • European and national regulatory authorities as required under applicable law

We maintain records of reports and cooperate fully with investigations by competent authorities.
